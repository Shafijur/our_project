Moving to the UK? This Test is One
of the Most Important Steps.

You'll need to pass the Life in the UK Test — a required step for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) or British Citizenship. It’s a key part of your immigration process.

If you're planning to make the UK your new home, there's one crucial step you can't afford to skip — the “Life in the UK Test.”

Whether you're applying for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) or British Citizenship, this test is a legal requirement and a key part of your immigration journey.

Whether you're applying for ILR or Citizenship, this test is a legal requirement.

What is the Life in the UK Test?

The Life in the UK Test is a computer-based exam designed to assess your understanding of British traditions, culture, history, laws, and values. It ensures that applicants have integrated into British society and are well-prepared for life in the UK.

Who Needs to Take the Test?

You’ll need to take the test if you are:

  • Applying for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)
  • Applying for British Citizenship
  • Between the ages of 18 and 65

You do not need to take the test if :

  • You are under 18 or over 65
  • You have a long-term physical or mental condition

The Life in the UK Test isn’t just a formality —
it’s your gateway to staying in the UK for good.

What’s in the Test?

  • Format: 24 multiple-choice questions
  • Duration: 45 minutes
  • Passing Score: You must answer at least 18 questions correctly (75%)

How to Prepare Effectively

Success depends on consistent and focused preparation. Here’s how to get started:

  1. Read the Official Handbook

    – This is the main study source. It’s available in print and digital formats.

  2. Use Online Practice Tests
    – Simulated exams can help you get used to the format and timing.
  3. Join a Preparation Course
    – Some training centres offer structured lessons to guide you through the material.
  4. Take Notes and Highlight Key Facts
    – Rewriting or summarising information can help reinforce your memory.
  5. Schedule Your Exam in Advance
    – Choose a nearby test centre and book early to avoid delays.

If You Fail?

Don’t worry — you can retake the test as many times as needed. However, you must pay the £50 fee for each attempt. Focus on improving your preparation before retaking it.

Final Thoughts

The Life in the UK Test might seem intimidating at first, but with the right resources and preparation, you can pass it with confidence. It’s not just about checking a box — it’s about proving your commitment to becoming part of British society.

Start preparing today, and take one step closer to calling the UK your new home
